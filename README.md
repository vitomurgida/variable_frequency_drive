# variable_frequency_drive
A simulator of a 6 pulses VFD

# Introduction and motivation
A variable frequency drive is a device allowing to control the frequency and power supplied to a rotating electromagnetic machine such as en elctric motor.

This code allows to simulate current and voltage across the different parts of a variable frequency drive (rectifier diodes, capacitor DC bus, PWM transistors, electric motor). The FFT of the different signals is computed and the frequency spectra are generated, allowing for a quantification of the signal distortion. 

As a further step, magnetic forces are assumed to depend quadratically from the currents, while acoustic noise generated by components is assumed to depend directly to magenetic forces. In such a way, acoustic harmonics can be also estimated.
